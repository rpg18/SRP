Summary

Single-cell technologies provide novel insight into individual cell types as well as evolution and cell life cycles. However, with it comes a large quantity of data to analyse. With a lack of standardised method, there remain many approaches to this. Darmanis et al. (2015) use this new technique to analyse the cells within the brain.

This website presents our attempt to re-analyse their data using the methodology they provide as well as creating a new and improved pipeline - PigeonPigs. We were able to reproduce the general conclusions of Darmanis et al. (2015), but not an exact replication.

The novel scRNAseq data analysis procedure PigeonPigs was able to reproduce much of their classification of cells into main brain cell types and discovered some new features in the dataset.

    Heatmap of Marker genes for 9 SC3 clusters

    Heatmap plot generated by sc3_plot_markers function with k = 9 from SC3, using the unfiltered matrix from PigeonPigs pipeline: The plot shows a maximum of ten marker genes per cluster, according to the total number of clusters. Here 9 clusters are selected. The log2 outlier score is represented in a green scale: from 0 to 4. These scores were calculated with the sc3_calc_biology function, which identifies outlier cells in the dataset. The cell type classification from Darmaris et al., (2015) is also shown. The marker genes for each cluster are represented as rows.
